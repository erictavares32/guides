Best Hidden Gems on Prime Video You Can't Find Everywhere (2025)

Prime Video's catalog varies dramatically by country, creating a global treasure trove of hidden gems. While most viewers only see their local selection, savvy streamers know there are incredible shows and movies quietly available in specific regions. From Dexter's prequel in India to Black Sails in Belgium, these 10 underrated titles represent the best of Prime Video's regional exclusives this year.

🌍 Top 10 Regional Prime Video Exclusives

Dexter: Original Sin ★ 4.6/5

Crime Drama Thriller 🇮🇳 Prime Video India

This gripping prequel explores Dexter Morgan's teenage years as he develops his infamous "code" under his father's guidance. Set in 1991 Miami, it reveals how a traumatized boy became TV's most fascinating serial killer. Surprisingly, this Paramount+ original is streaming exclusively on Prime Video in India, making it a rare find for international Dexter fans. The young Dexter (Patrick Gibson) perfectly captures Michael C. Hall's mannerisms while bringing fresh vulnerability to the role.

Why Watch: A fresh take on the Dexter mythology that stands on its own while rewarding longtime fans with clever callbacks.

Black Sails ★ 4.8/5

Adventure Drama Historical 🇧🇪 🇳🇱 Prime Video Belgium & Netherlands

This underrated gem blends historical piracy with Treasure Island lore, creating a sophisticated drama that rivals Game of Thrones in political intrigue. Toby Stephens shines as Captain Flint, whose quest for the ultimate score threatens to unravel his crew. The naval battles are among TV's most realistic, and the complex characters defy pirate stereotypes. Despite being a Starz original, it's only available on Prime Video in select European countries, making it a true hidden treasure.

Why Watch: One of television's most meticulously researched historical dramas, with writing that elevates pirate fiction to high art.

Prodigal Son ★ 4.4/5

Crime Psychological Thriller 🇯🇵 Prime Video Japan

Michael Sheen delivers a chilling performance as Dr. Martin Whitly, a notorious serial killer whose son (Tom Payne) becomes an FBI profiler. The twisted father-son dynamic fuels this gripping procedural, which balances gruesome cases with dark humor and family drama. Despite critical praise, the show was canceled after two seasons and is now surprisingly available only on Prime Video Japan - a hidden gem for crime drama enthusiasts.

Why Watch: Sheen's mesmerizing performance as "The Surgeon" makes this one of TV's most compelling serial killer portrayals.

Power ★ 4.5/5

Crime Drama Urban 🇫🇷 Prime Video France

50 Cent executive produces this gripping saga about James "Ghost" St. Patrick (Omari Hardwick), a nightclub owner living a double life as a drug kingpin. The show's authentic portrayal of New York's underworld and its Shakespearean power struggles made it a cultural phenomenon. While originally a Starz series, it's currently streaming exclusively on Prime Video France - an unexpected home for this American crime epic.

Why Watch: A masterclass in tension and character development that revitalized urban crime drama for prestige TV audiences.

Hacks ★ 4.7/5

Comedy Drama Female-led 🇮🇪 🇬🇧 Prime Video Ireland & UK

Jean Smart is sublime as Deborah Vance, a legendary Las Vegas comedian forced to reinvent her act, with Hannah Einbinder as the Gen Z writer hired to help. This HBO Max original surprisingly streams on Prime Video in the UK and Ireland, offering international viewers a chance to experience one of TV's sharpest comedies about show business, aging, and creative collaboration. The writing crackles with wit while delivering surprising emotional depth.

Why Watch: Jean Smart's career-best performance in a comedy that's both hilarious and heartbreakingly real about women in entertainment.

Fargo ★ 4.9/5

Crime Anthology Dark Comedy 🇨🇦 🇫🇷 🇮🇹 🇪🇸 Prime Video Canada, France, Italy & Spain

Each season of this Coen Brothers-inspired anthology presents a new Midwestern crime saga with stellar casts (Billy Bob Thornton, Kirsten Dunst, Ewan McGregor). The show's blend of quirky humor and sudden violence stays true to the original film's spirit while expanding the universe. Oddly unavailable on U.S. Prime Video, it's a hidden gem for international subscribers who appreciate sophisticated crime storytelling.

Why Watch: Some of television's most inventive crime narratives, with each season offering a self-contained masterpiece.

Babygirl ★ 4.3/5

Drama Coming-of-Age Indie 🇨🇦 🇧🇷 🇮🇳 🇲🇽 Prime Video Canada, Brazil, India & Mexico

This intimate indie drama follows a young woman navigating motherhood, identity, and generational trauma. With raw performances and poetic cinematography, it's the type of thoughtful character study that often gets overlooked in the streaming era. Currently available on Prime Video in several international markets but not the U.S., it's a perfect example of the platform's hidden cinematic gems.

Why Watch: A beautifully observed character piece that showcases emerging talent both in front of and behind the camera.

The Whale ★ 4.8/5

Drama Psychological Adaptation 🇩🇪 🇫🇷 🇯🇵 Prime Video Germany, France & Japan

Brendan Fraser's Oscar-winning performance anchors this heartbreaking story of a reclusive English teacher attempting to reconnect with his estranged daughter. Directed by Darren Aronofsky, the film's claustrophobic intensity and emotional honesty make it a modern character study masterpiece. Surprisingly unavailable on U.S. Prime Video, it's currently streaming in several international markets where it's become a word-of-mouth hit.

Why Watch: Fraser's triumphant return in one of the most powerful performances of the decade.

Fast X ★ 4.1/5

Action Adventure Crime 🇦🇹 🇩🇪 🇲🇽 🇪🇸 Prime Video Austria, Germany, Mexico & Spain

The tenth installment in the Fast & Furious franchise delivers the series' signature over-the-top action as Dom Toretto (Vin Diesel) faces his most personal threat yet. With spectacular set pieces and returning favorites like Jason Momoa's flamboyant villain, it's pure blockbuster entertainment. While not available on Prime Video in all countries, it's a hidden gem for action fans in select markets where it streams.

Why Watch: Peak Fast & Furious insanity with some of the franchise's most inventive action sequences.

Mission: Impossible – Dead Reckoning Part One ★ 4.7/5

Action Spy Thriller 🇺🇸 Prime Video USA

Tom Cruise returns as Ethan Hunt in this jaw-dropping installment featuring the franchise's most dangerous stunt yet (a motorcycle cliff jump). The story introduces a terrifying AI villain while bringing back familiar faces. While most major markets have this on Paramount+, it's currently a Prime Video exclusive in the U.S. - making it a rare blockbuster gem on the platform.

Why Watch: State-of-the-art action filmmaking with Cruise pushing physical limits in ways that redefine the spy genre.

Frequently Asked Questions About Prime Video Regional Exclusives

Why can't I find these shows on my Prime Video?

Due to licensing agreements, streaming platforms offer different content libraries in each country. A show might be on Netflix in one country, Prime Video in another, and unavailable elsewhere. We verify regional availability monthly to ensure our recommendations are accurate.

Will these shows eventually come to other countries?

Licensing deals typically last 1-3 years, so availability can change. Some titles may eventually appear in more regions, while others remain exclusive to certain markets indefinitely. We recommend checking periodically if a particular hidden gem becomes available in your country.

Are there legal ways to watch these regional exclusives?

Yes! Many titles are available for digital purchase on platforms like iTunes or Amazon Video in countries where they aren't included with Prime. Some may also be on DVD/Blu-ray with international shipping options. Always support content through official channels when possible.

How do you discover these hidden regional gems?

Our team monitors global streaming catalogs using specialized tools and local sources. We focus on quality titles that deserve wider recognition but are geographically restricted. Each recommendation is hand-picked based on critical reception and audience feedback.

Which country has the most hidden gems on Prime Video?

Currently, Prime Video India and Japan offer the most surprising exclusives relative to their size. Smaller European markets like Belgium and Portugal also frequently get overlooked content. The U.S. library, while large, misses many international shows that appear elsewhere.
