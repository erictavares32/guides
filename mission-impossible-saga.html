The Complete Mission: Impossible Saga (1996-2025)

For nearly three decades, Tom Cruise's Ethan Hunt has defined the spy thriller genre with death-defying stunts and intricate espionage plots. This comprehensive guide covers all eight Mission: Impossible films - from Brian De Palma's 1996 original to Christopher McQuarrie's 2025 finale - with plot summaries, director insights, streaming availability, and franchise analysis.

Mission: Impossible (1996)

Director: Brian De Palma 1996

Available on Paramount+, Amazon Prime

The film that launched the franchise sees Ethan Hunt (Tom Cruise) framed as a mole after his IMF team is ambushed during a mission to recover a stolen CIA NOC list. Featuring the iconic CIA vault heist sequence and a twist-filled narrative, De Palma's thriller established the series' blend of spycraft and action. The Prague mission remains one of cinema's most memorable opening sequences, while the film's central mystery set the template for the franchise's signature betrayals.

Franchise Analysis & Key Themes

The Evolution of Ethan Hunt

From the relatively green agent in 1996 to the weary but determined operative in 2025, Ethan Hunt's character arc is one of cinema's most compelling action hero journeys. Unlike many franchise protagonists, Ethan visibly ages and changes, with each mission taking a physical and emotional toll. The later films particularly explore his isolation and the personal sacrifices demanded by his work.
