10 Gripping Shows Like Netflix's You

If you're fascinated by You's blend of psychological thriller, dark romance, and unreliable narration, these series deliver similarly addictive stories about obsession, dangerous relationships, and the masks people wear. From serial killers to manipulative lovers, these shows explore the dark side of human connection that makes You so compelling.

Dexter

Showtime

Available on Showtime, Paramount+

Dexter follows a blood spatter analyst with a secret life as a vigilante serial killer. Like Joe Goldberg in You, Dexter Morgan narrates his dark thoughts while maintaining a normal facade. The show's exploration of a killer's psychology and moral code makes it the closest counterpart to You's disturbing charm. Michael C. Hall's performance balances menace and relatability much like Penn Badgley's portrayal of Joe.

Bates Motel

Peacock

Available on Peacock

This Psycho prequel explores Norman Bates' twisted relationship with his mother. Like You, it features a sympathetic yet terrifying protagonist whose mental state deteriorates over time. Freddie Highmore's portrayal of Norman mirrors Joe Goldberg's descent, with both characters justifying increasingly horrific actions. The show's exploration of mother-son codependency offers similarly uncomfortable fascination as You's toxic relationships.

The Fall

Amazon Prime

Available on Amazon Prime

Gillian Anderson stars as a detective hunting Jamie Dornan's serial killer in this cat-and-mouse thriller. The Fall shares You's dual perspective structure, alternating between hunter and hunted. Paul Spector's family-man facade and secret life parallel Joe Goldberg's deceptions. The show's slow-burn tension and psychological depth make it essential for You fans who enjoy complex character studies of predators.

Mindhunter

Netflix

Available on Netflix

David Fincher's Mindhunter delves into the psychology of serial killers through FBI interviews. While more procedural than You, it shares the fascination with what makes predators tick. The show's unsettling atmosphere and focus on manipulation tactics (similar to Joe's gaslighting) make it compelling for fans of psychological depth. Its historical basis adds chilling realism to the study of criminal minds.

Behind Her Eyes

Netflix

Available on Netflix

This twisty thriller follows a single mother who becomes entangled with a married couple. Behind Her Eyes shares You's themes of obsession and dangerous secrets, with a similarly unreliable narrative perspective. The limited series format allows for a tightly plotted story with shocking reveals that rival You's biggest twists. Its exploration of psychological manipulation makes it perfect for fans of mind games.

The Patient

Hulu

Available on Hulu

Steve Carell plays a therapist held captive by his serial killer patient. The Patient explores the dynamic between a manipulator and their target, similar to You's power struggles. The confined setting creates intense psychological drama, focusing on how people rationalize evil. Like Joe Goldberg, the killer here believes he can change, creating disturbing sympathy for a monster.

Dirty John

Netflix

Available on Netflix

Based on true events, Dirty John follows a charismatic con artist who infiltrates a wealthy family. The show shares You's themes of deception in relationships and the danger of charming outsiders. Eric Bana's John Meehan is a real-world counterpart to Joe Goldberg, using manipulation rather than violence to control his victims. The true crime basis adds chilling realism to the story.

Younger

Hulu

Available on Hulu

While lighter in tone, Younger explores identity deception like You, with a 40-year-old woman pretending to be in her 20s. The show's examination of how lies compound and the masks people wear makes it an interesting thematic companion. Sutton Foster's Liza navigates romantic entanglements while maintaining her facade, similar to Joe's balancing act, though with more comedy than horror.

Sharp Objects

HBO Max

Available on HBO Max

Amy Adams stars as a troubled journalist investigating murders in her hometown. Sharp Objects shares You's themes of psychological trauma and unreliable narration. The limited series format allows for deep character study, with Camille's self-destructive tendencies mirroring Joe's. Its Southern Gothic atmosphere creates a similarly oppressive mood, where past sins haunt the present.

Killing Eve

AMC+

Available on AMC+, Hulu

This cat-and-mouse thriller follows an intelligence agent and the assassin obsessed with her. Killing Eve shares You's themes of dangerous obsession and blurred lines between hunter and prey. Sandra Oh and Jodie Comer's electric performances create a twisted dynamic similar to Joe's relationships. The show's dark humor and stylish violence make it perfect for You fans who enjoy charismatic killers.

Key Themes in These Psychological Thrillers

Obsessive Relationships

Like You, these shows explore dangerous fixations - from Dexter's need to kill to Villanelle's obsession with Eve in Killing Eve. They examine how obsession corrupts both the hunter and the hunted.

Unreliable Narrators

Many feature protagonists who lie to themselves and viewers, similar to Joe Goldberg's self-justifications. Norman Bates in Bates Motel and Camille in Sharp Objects present distorted versions of reality.

Dual Identities

Characters lead double lives - Dexter the killer/forensic expert, Liza the older woman/young professional in Younger. Like Joe, they maintain facades while hiding darker selves.

Psychological Manipulation

Gaslighting, mind games, and emotional abuse feature prominently, especially in The Fall, Behind Her Eyes, and Dirty John. These shows reveal how predators exploit vulnerabilities.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes these shows similar to You?

These series share You's core elements: obsessive relationships, unreliable narrators, psychological manipulation, and dark secrets. They all feature complex characters who blur the line between protagonist and antagonist, with narratives that explore the darker aspects of love and human psychology. Many use direct address narration like You's internal monologues, creating uncomfortable intimacy with disturbed minds.

Which show is most like You's stalker premise?

Dexter has the most similar premise, featuring a protagonist with a dark secret life who justifies his actions. The Fall also offers a comparable cat-and-mouse dynamic between hunter and hunted. Behind Her Eyes shares You's themes of obsession and hidden identities, while Bates Motel provides another sympathetic-yet-terrifying lead character whose perspective we follow.

Are there any shows with female antiheroes like You?

Yes, Killing Eve features two complex female leads in a dangerous game of mutual obsession. Sharp Objects follows a troubled female protagonist with dark secrets and self-destructive tendencies. Dirty John: The Betty Broderick Story explores female rage and obsession in relationships, while Behind Her Eyes has female characters with hidden agendas comparable to Love Quinn in You.

Which shows have the best psychological depth?

Mindhunter offers unparalleled psychological insight into criminal minds through FBI profiling. Bates Motel provides a deep character study of Norman Bates' descent into madness. The Patient explores therapy dynamics with a serial killer patient, while Sharp Objects delves into intergenerational trauma and addiction with remarkable nuance.
