10 Must-Watch Series If You Loved Netflix’s 'You'

Dexter

Dexter follows Dexter Morgan, a forensic expert who leads a double life as a vigilante serial killer. His internal struggle between justice and his dark urges creates a compelling narrative that delves deep into the psyche of a killer with a code.

Bates Motel

Bates Motel serves as a contemporary prequel to Hitchcock's 'Psycho', exploring the twisted relationship between Norman Bates and his mother, Norma. The series delves into Norman's descent into madness, offering a chilling portrayal of a killer's origin.

The Fall

The Fall pits Detective Stella Gibson against Paul Spector, a family man leading a double life as a serial killer. This psychological thriller intricately examines the minds of both hunter and hunted, blurring the lines between good and evil.

Mindhunter

Mindhunter delves into the early days of criminal psychology and criminal profiling at the FBI. Agents Holden Ford and Bill Tench interview imprisoned serial killers to understand their psychology, laying the groundwork for modern serial crime investigations.

Behind Her Eyes

Behind Her Eyes is a psychological thriller that follows a single mother who enters a world of twisted mind games when she begins an affair with her psychiatrist boss while secretly befriending his mysterious wife. Secrets unravel in this suspenseful tale.

The Patient

The Patient centers on a therapist who is held captive by a serial killer seeking help to curb his homicidal urges. The intense psychological drama explores themes of captivity, morality, and the human capacity for change.

Dirty John

Dirty John is an anthology series that dramatizes true crime stories of love gone wrong. The first season follows Debra Newell's relationship with the charismatic John Meehan, whose facade hides a dark past and dangerous intentions.

Younger

Younger follows Liza Miller, a 40-year-old woman who pretends to be in her 20s to re-enter the competitive publishing industry. While lighter in tone, the series explores themes of identity, deception, and the complexities of modern relationships.

Sharp Objects

Sharp Objects follows journalist Camille Preaker as she returns to her hometown to cover a series of brutal murders. Confronting her own traumatic past, the series delves into themes of psychological scars and the darkness lurking beneath the surface.

Killing Eve

Killing Eve centers on Eve Polastri, a British intelligence investigator, and Villanelle, a skilled assassin. Their mutual obsession leads to a thrilling game of cat and mouse
