Top 10 Disney+ Shows & Movies You Must Watch in 2025

Disney+ continues to dominate family streaming in 2025 with an impressive mix of Star Wars, Marvel, Pixar, and original content. From the gritty Andor Season 2 to the time-traveling adventures of Doctor Who, and from the animated emotions of Inside Out 2 to the R-rated chaos of Deadpool & Wolverine, this guide covers the essential Disney+ content worth streaming this year.

Andor (Season 2) ‚òÖ 4.9/5

Sci-Fi Political Thriller Star Wars

The final season of Tony Gilroy's acclaimed Star Wars series escalates the rebellion with higher stakes and deeper intrigue. Diego Luna's Cassian Andor completes his transformation into the committed operative we meet in Rogue One, while Genevieve O'Reilly's Mon Mothma navigates increasing political danger. The 12-episode season is structured in four three-episode blocks, each representing one year closer to Rogue One's events. With its mature storytelling, complex characters, and stunning production design, Andor remains the most sophisticated Star Wars television project to date.

Why Watch: A masterclass in spy storytelling that happens to be set in the Star Wars universe, offering television's most nuanced take on rebellion and sacrifice.

Doctor Who (Season 2) ‚òÖ 4.7/5

Sci-Fi Adventure Time Travel

Ncuti Gatwa settles fully into the role of the Fifteenth Doctor in this second Disney+-era season, accompanied by Millie Gibson as companion Ruby Sunday. Showrunner Russell T Davies delivers ambitious standalone adventures alongside an evolving story arc involving mysterious forces manipulating the Doctor's timeline. The increased budget allows for more cinematic episodes, including a stunning space station disaster story and a historical episode set in 18th-century Venice. Gatwa's charismatic, emotionally vulnerable Doctor continues to redefine the character while honoring the show's 60-year legacy.

Why Watch: Classic Doctor Who reinvented for a global audience with higher production values and one of the most compelling Doctor-companion dynamics in years.

Daredevil: Born Again ‚òÖ 4.6/5

Superhero Action Crime Drama

Charlie Cox returns as Matt Murdock in this soft reboot that brings the fan-favorite character fully into the MCU while maintaining the gritty tone of the original Netflix series. The 18-episode season explores Matt's struggle to balance his legal career with his vigilante activities as Wilson Fisk (Vincent D'Onofrio) consolidates power in New York. New characters include White Tiger and returning favorites like Foggy Nelson (Elden Henson) and Karen Page (Deborah Ann Woll). The fight choreography maintains the brutal, practical style that made the original series stand out.

Why Watch: Marvel's best street-level hero returns with the same grounded intensity that made the original series a hit, now with deeper MCU connections.

Tucci in Italy ‚òÖ 4.8/5

Travel Culinary Documentary

Stanley Tucci's beloved culinary travelogue returns for a second season exploring Italy's diverse regional cuisines. This installment features deeper dives into food traditions, with episodes dedicated to Tuscany's rustic simplicity, Lombardy's rich butter-based dishes, and Lazio's iconic Roman cuisine. Tucci's warm narration and genuine curiosity make even obscure culinary techniques fascinating, while his celebrity guests (including his wife Felicity Blunt) add personal perspectives. The stunning cinematography captures both Italy's landscapes and the artistry of its chefs, making this a feast for eyes and imagination.

Why Watch: The most delicious show on Disney+, offering equal parts wanderlust and food appreciation through Tucci's charming perspective.

Ironheart ‚òÖ 4.3/5

Superhero Sci-Fi Drama

Dominique Thorne reprises her role as Riri Williams, the genius MIT student who builds her own version of Iron Man armor. Set after the events of Black Panther: Wakanda Forever, the series explores Riri's struggles to balance school life with superhero responsibilities as she faces off against The Hood (Anthony Ramos), a magic-wielding crime boss. The show delves into Marvel's technomagic lore while maintaining a coming-of-age focus, with Riri's friendships and family relationships providing emotional grounding. The armor designs showcase impressive CGI, particularly in aerial combat sequences.

Why Watch: A fresh perspective on the Marvel universe through a young, brilliant protagonist who isn't just "Iron Man but female."

Deadpool & Wolverine ‚òÖ 4.5/5

Superhero Action Comedy

Ryan Reynolds and Hugh Jackman team up in this R-rated MCU debut that breaks the fourth wall while breaking bones. The plot involves the Time Variance Authority recruiting Deadpool for a multiversal mission that requires Wolverine's help, leading to constant bickering and spectacular action. Director Shawn Levy balances crude humor with genuine character moments, particularly in exploring Wolverine's world-weariness versus Deadpool's irreverence. Cameos from other Marvel properties (including some surprise X-Men variants) make this a must-watch for longtime fans, while the central duo's chemistry ensures broad appeal.

Why Watch: The most purely fun Marvel movie in years, delivering on its promise of chaotic team-ups and meta humor while advancing the MCU's multiverse saga.

Captain America: Brave New World ‚òÖ 4.4/5

Superhero Action Political Thriller

Anthony Mackie's Sam Wilson fully embraces the Captain America mantle in this geopolitical thriller that sees the new Cap navigating complex international tensions. The story involves a biological weapon threat and the return of Tim Blake Nelson's The Leader from The Incredible Hulk, creating a crisis that tests Sam's ideals. The film explores what Captain America means in a divided modern world, with Mackie bringing gravitas and charm to the role. Action sequences highlight Sam's aerial combat skills with the wingsuit, while supporting characters like Joaquin Torres (Danny Ramirez) and Isaiah Bradley (Carl Lumbly) add depth to the Captain America legacy.

Why Watch: A thoughtful examination of heroism in contemporary society, featuring Marvel's most grounded action since Winter Soldier.

The Abyss (Remastered) ‚òÖ 4.7/5

Sci-Fi Thriller Adventure

James Cameron's 1989 underwater classic receives a stunning 4K remaster with enhanced visual effects, making its Disney+ debut the definitive way to experience this groundbreaking film. The story of deep-sea oil rig workers encountering mysterious aquatic aliens holds up remarkably well, with Ed Harris and Mary Elizabeth Mastrantonio's performances adding human drama to the spectacle. The remaster particularly improves the third act's alien sequences and the iconic "liquid oxygen" scene, while Hans Zimmer's score has been meticulously restored. The film's themes of first contact and Cold War tensions feel newly relevant in today's climate.

Why Watch: A chance to experience Cameron's visionary filmmaking as it was meant to be seen, with technical improvements that enhance its timeless story.

The Marvels ‚òÖ 4.2/5

Superhero Action Space Adventure

Brie Larson, Teyonah Parris, and Iman Vellani team up in this cosmic adventure that sees Captain Marvel, Monica Rambeau, and Ms. Marvel swapping places whenever they use their light-based powers. The film balances large-scale space battles with character-driven humor, particularly in Kamala Khan's fan-girl interactions with Carol Danvers. Director Nia DaCosta brings visual flair to the power-swapping sequences and creates inventive action scenes that showcase the trio's combined abilities. The story sets up important developments for the Marvel cosmic universe while standing as an entertaining standalone adventure.

Why Watch: Marvel's most fun team-up since Guardians of the Galaxy, with three charismatic leads whose chemistry elevates the material.

Frequently Asked Questions About Disney+ 2025 Releases

What are the best family-friendly options on Disney+ in 2025?

For family viewing, we recommend Inside Out 2 (Pixar's emotional sequel), The Marvels (lighthearted superhero team-up), and the remastered Abyss (classic sci-fi adventure). Older kids may also enjoy Doctor Who and Ironheart, though parents should note Deadpool & Wolverine is R-rated.

How does Daredevil: Born Again connect to the Netflix series?

Daredevil: Born Again is considered canon to the original Netflix series but designed as a fresh starting point. Key characters and events from the Netflix show are referenced, but new viewers can follow along without prior knowledge. The tone remains similarly gritty despite being on Disney+.

Is Andor Season 2 more action-packed than Season 1?

While Andor Season 2 features more overt rebellion action as it approaches Rogue One's timeline, it maintains Season 1's focus on espionage and political maneuvering. The tension builds gradually across its 12 episodes, culminating in events that directly connect to the film's opening scenes.

What makes the Disney+ Doctor Who different from BBC versions?

The Disney+ Doctor Who features higher production values (allowing for more ambitious locations and effects), global distribution, and some creative input from Disney, though the show remains fundamentally British in sensibility under Russell T Davies' leadership.

Will there be more Tucci in Italy seasons?

While not officially confirmed, the popularity of Tucci in Italy makes additional seasons likely. Stanley Tucci has expressed interest in exploring Sicily, Sardinia, and Italy's lesser-known regions in future installments.
